# Abstract

In May 2026, the Global AI Token Tracker (GATT) measures **561,200 tokens per United States resident per day** — 2.49× the 2028 physical ceiling of 225,000 tokens per person per day projected by Litowitz, Polson, and Sokolov (2026, arXiv:2603.06630) under a 326 TWh AI energy allocation. This paper presents the empirical methodology underlying that measurement and proposes three reconciliation interpretations: (i) GATT may overestimate top vendors via attribution multipliers — though the Gemini All-Surfaces multiplier was disclosed (not editorial) at the Google I/O 2026 keynote of May 19-20 [42], eliminating the editorial-judgment dependency for the largest US vendor; (ii) the paper's Landauer/Shannon ceiling is conservative because real 2026 inference uses Mixture-of-Experts architectures, 4-bit quantization, speculative decoding, and dedicated inference ASICs, yielding 5-10× more tokens per Joule than the physics baseline; and (iii) the two metrics measure different units — GATT measures empirical all-surface output, while the physical-ceiling paper models theoretical capacity assuming fresh compute per token.

GATT is, to our knowledge, the first global, all-sources, daily-updated index of AI token throughput, covering 24 vendors across 12 countries. As of May 20, 2026 (post-Google-I/O 2026), GATT measures global daily output at 345 trillion tokens, with the United States re-taking the volume lead (55% to China's 45%) after Pichai's I/O 2026 keynote disclosed 3.2 quadrillion Gemini tokens/month across all Google surfaces [42] — a 7× year-over-year increase from approximately 480 trillion/month at I/O 2025, equivalent to roughly 107 trillion tokens/day all-surfaces and 27.4 trillion/day via direct API (19 billion tokens/minute, up from 16 billion at Cloud Next April). The United States captures 90% of "Token GDP" — a vendor-volume × blended-pricing metric — due to a 15× pricing asymmetry between US and Chinese vendors; annualized global Token GDP stands at $115.6 billion. The US-India per-capita gap stands at 729×; the population-weighted Gini coefficient of token consumption across the 12 countries is **0.698**, already exceeding global income inequality (~0.62) and approaching wealth-distribution levels of concentration. We document our methodology, contrast it with IDC China Public Cloud MaaS (which independently confirms our top vendor ranking at 49.5% Volcengine market share), and propose Token GDP as a regional-comparable economic-value metric for the emerging Token Economy.
