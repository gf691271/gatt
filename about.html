About GATT

Global AI Token Tracker — tracking AI inference as commodity, currency, and geopolitical force.

Who

Frank Gao is a tech communications strategist with 15 years of experience at Alibaba, Microsoft, Nokia, and HONOR. GATT started as a personal project to make AI infrastructure data accessible and citable — because the most consequential shift in global compute is happening without a public scoreboard.

Why This Exists

AI inference is the new commodity. Every day, the world's AI systems process hundreds of trillions of tokens — yet there's no Bloomberg Terminal for this data. No standardized index. No one tracking it systematically.

GATT introduces the concept of the Token Economy: the global system of AI inference production, consumption, and exchange. Tokens are the atomic unit of AI work — and they're becoming as economically significant as barrels of oil or kilowatt-hours of electricity.

Extrapolation Method

When the latest data point is older than 30 days, we extrapolate using compound monthly growth rates derived from at least 2 historical data points. Growth rates are capped and adjusted downward when maturation signals appear.

Scope: "All Surfaces" vs "API Only"

For vendors like Google who report separate metrics, GATT uses the All Surfaces scope for apple-to-apple comparison with ByteDance (who reports total service volume).

Dollar-Equivalent Compute (DEC)

Because a token from Doubao costs ~$0.14/T while a token from Claude costs ~$5/T, raw counts don't tell the full story. DEC reveals that the US leads in compute value despite China leading in token volume.

What IDC Says (May 2026)

The IDC China Public Cloud Large Model Services report (May 7, 2026) finds China's external enterprise MaaS market grew from 114T tokens (2024) → 1,944T (2025, +16× YoY), with a 2026 forecast of 40,000T (+20× YoY). Volcengine ranks #1 at 49.5% market share (full-year 2025), followed by Alibaba Bailian #2 (27%) and Baidu Qianfan #3 (17%, H1 2025).

How IDC's 49.5% Validates — and Differs From — GATT's 129T/day Doubao

IDC independently confirms Volcengine's #1 ranking in China — same direction as GATT's Doubao #1 globally.

But the absolute numbers differ in scope. IDC's 2025 China total of 1,944T tokens averages to 5.32T/day for the entire external MaaS market. Volcengine's external MaaS share at 49.5% would be roughly 2.64T/day in 2025. The Volcengine official disclosure of 120T/day at the April 1, 2026 Wuhan AI Innovation Expo cannot fit IDC's external-only scope under any plausible growth path.

The reconciliation: GATT's 129T/day Doubao number is inherently all-sources — external MaaS calls plus internal first-party usage from Douyin AI Search, Doubao App, Jimeng, and Volcengine internal pipelines. Internal usage is estimated at ≥95% of total Volcengine throughput.

Both measures matter. IDC's external MaaS share is the right metric for cloud business competitive position. GATT's all-sources volume is the right metric for total AI inference scale, geopolitical implications, and per-capita AI intensity.

Why GATT Exists When IDC Does

IDC's MaaS report covers China only. There is no equivalent IDC token-volume report for the US, Europe, or rest of world — global research firms publish in USD (infra spending, AI market sizing), not tokens. GATT fills this gap by aggregating per-vendor official disclosures (OpenAI's 15B tokens/min, Pichai's 16B tokens/min, Volcengine's 120T/day) into a single global, daily, comparable index.
